  • Solar Panels: Panel Cleaning (6-12 months); System Inspection (Yearly); Inverter Replacement (~10-15 years); Panel Replacement (~25-30 years).

  • Low-E Windows: Seal Inspection (every 5-10 years); Potential Unit Replacement (20-30 years).
  • Greywater Recycling System: Filter Cleaning/Replacement (1-3 months); Pump Inspection (Yearly); Tank Desludging (1-3 years).
  • Sustainable Timber Cladding: Cleaning/Inspection (Yearly); Re-coating/Sealing (every 2-5 years depending on product/exposure).
  • Clay Roof Tiles: Visual Inspection (Yearly); Underlayment/Flashing Check (5-10 years, potential replacement 20-30 yrs).
  • Recycled Brick Walls: Mortar Inspection (5-10 years); Repointing needed (~20-50 years).

4. 🧠 Recommendations

  • Enhance Timber Cladding Protection: Utilize a highly durable tropical timber species certified for sustainability, or apply high-performance penetrating wood treatment and a schedule for bi-annual re-application of UV-blocking sealant. Design roof overhangs to provide additional protection from sun and rain.
  • UV Protect Exposed PVC: All external PVC piping must be coated with UV-resistant paint or shielded to maximize lifespan beyond the typical 10-20 years for exposed PVC in high solar radiation environments.
  • Optimize Solar Panel Setup: Select solar panels with a low temperature coefficient for better performance in high heat (LST: 33.7°C). Ensure mounting allows ample airflow underneath panels to dissipate heat.
  • Improve Roof System Resilience: Implement ridge and soffit vents to encourage passive ventilation within the roof cavity, mitigating heat buildup and moisture condensation which can affect insulation and structure. Verify roof underlayment is rated for high temperature and humidity.
  • Bolster Window Seals & Shading: Specify window seals explicitly designed for tropical climates with high UV and heat resistance. Integrate external shading elements (e.g., architectural fins, operable screens) to reduce direct solar heat gain on windows, prolonging seal life and boosting thermal comfort.
  • Refine Water Systems Filtration: Add multi-stage pre-filtration (leaf guards, first-flush diverters) to both rainwater and greywater systems to handle high rainfall debris loads and reduce maintenance frequency on finer filters and pumps. Specify corrosion-resistant materials (e.g., stainless steel) for pump impellers and fittings.
  • Seal Masonry Walls: After full mortar cure, apply a quality breathable (silane/siloxane-based) sealant to brick walls to minimize water absorption and efflorescence risk during heavy rainfall, without trapping internal moisture.

5. ✅ Final Durability Verdict

Overall Rating: Good
The core structural materials (concrete foundation, recycled brick walls, clay roof tiles) offer excellent intrinsic durability suitable for the tropical climate. However, the high heat, intense solar radiation, and heavy rainfall pose significant challenges to the timber cladding, exposed PVC components, and the operational longevity of system components like window seals, solar panel efficiency, and water system pumps/filters. The 'Good' rating is contingent upon diligent adherence to the recommended maintenance schedules, particularly for the timber cladding and water recycling systems, and implementation of protective measures like UV coatings and enhanced ventilation/shading.
